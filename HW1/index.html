  • The graph illustrate a list of sponsors for Nike from across industries. It also visually ranks the sponsors by sponsorship size. There are only 3 colors in the graph: orange, black and grey. Even better, the Nike logo is at the center of the graph.
  • This is a GOOD visualization because it has very few color, few words, but has powerful information. The small figures also visually illustrate what sports it sponsored.The only two important numbers are big and obvious.
  • The author tries to convey that Nike's has sponsored not only a large amount of money in sports, but also in various number of sports. The message is concise and powerful.
  • Source: viz.wtf and Personal Finance

  • The graph illustrate the number of infection of Zombism across the US. It uses Gillespie dynamics on block-level census dtata from 2010 using 308 million people interacting across the continental US. There are two colors in the graph: black and white. Each data point represents on infection. The lighter the graph the more frequent the infection happens.
  • This is a GOOD visualization because it has very few color, almost no words, but has powerful information. We can easily tell where most infection happens. More importantly, the graph looks very pretty - like a piece of art. two
  • The author tries to convey that such disease happens in areas where the most population is. This information is very clear!
  • Source: Matt Bierbaum's zombie map

  • The graph describs customer support activity for 18 weeks. The information in the graph includes number of days, number of issues. The activity is also listed by type: new, active and closed. The customers are also grouped by age. There are 8 different colors in the graph.
  • This is a BAD visualization because there are too many information in the graph and are hard to distinguish from one another.
  • The author wants to illustrate all the data on customer support activities on a weekly basis. However, there is too many data points on one single chart. I suggest the author either to use a few charts with linkages or to use a interactive chart. Less color and less data points will tell a better story.
  • Source: viz.wtf
